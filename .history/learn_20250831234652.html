Understanding Money Basics

Learn the fundamental concepts about money that everyone should know.

What is Money?

Money is anything that is widely accepted in exchange for goods and services. Throughout history, people have used many things as money including shells, beads, precious metals, and now paper currency and digital money.

Money serves three main purposes:

  • Medium of exchange: Used to buy and sell goods
  • Store of value: Holds its value over time
  • Unit of account: Measures the value of goods and services

Key Financial Concepts

Income vs. Expenses

Income is money you receive, while expenses are money you spend. To build wealth, your income should be greater than your expenses.

Needs vs. Wants

Needs are essential for survival (food, shelter), while wants are things we desire but can live without. Learning to distinguish between them is key to good money management.

Interest

Interest is the cost of borrowing money or the reward for saving money. When you save, you earn interest. When you borrow, you pay interest.

Saving & Budgeting

Learn how to manage your money effectively through saving and budgeting.

The Importance of Saving

Saving means setting aside money for future use instead of spending it all now. People save for various reasons:

  • Emergency funds for unexpected expenses
  • Large purchases (bike, computer, car)
  • Future goals (college, travel, home)
  • Retirement (when you're older and not working)

A good rule for saving is the 50-30-20 rule: 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings.

Budgeting Techniques

Envelope System

Divide cash into envelopes for different spending categories. When an envelope is empty, you stop spending in that category.

Budgeting Apps

Use technology to track your spending and saving. Many apps can help you visualize where your money goes each month.

Pay Yourself First

When you receive money, immediately set aside your savings before spending on anything else. This ensures you always save.

The Power of Compound Interest

Compound interest is when you earn interest on both your original savings and on the interest you've already earned. Over time, this can make your money grow much faster!

Example: If you save $100 at 5% interest per year:

  • Year 1: $100 + $5 interest = $105
  • Year 2: $105 + $5.25 interest = $110.25
  • Year 3: $110.25 + $5.51 interest = $115.76

The earlier you start saving, the more time compound interest has to work its magic!

Investing & Funds

Learn how to make your money work for you through investing.

What is Investing?

Investing means using your money to buy assets that have the potential to grow in value over time. While saving protects your money, investing helps it grow.

Common types of investments include:

  • Stocks - Ownership shares in a company
  • Bonds - Loans to companies or governments
  • Mutual Funds - Collections of stocks and bonds
  • Real Estate - Property and land

Understanding Investment Funds

Mutual Funds

Pools money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. Managed by professionals.

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)

Similar to mutual funds but trade like stocks on exchanges. Often have lower fees than mutual funds.

Index Funds

A type of mutual fund or ETF that aims to match the performance of a specific market index (like the S&P 500).

Risk and Return

All investments involve some risk. Generally, investments with higher potential returns also come with higher risk. It's important to:

  • Diversify - Spread your investments across different types of assets
  • Invest for the long term - The stock market goes up and down, but historically it has increased in value over long periods
  • Understand your risk tolerance - How comfortable are you with the possibility of losing money?

Financial Terms Glossary

Budget

A plan for how you will spend your money, balancing income and expenses.

Interest

The cost of borrowing money or the earnings on saved or invested money.

Stock

A share of ownership in a company. If the company does well, the stock value may increase.

Diversification

Spreading investments across different types of assets to reduce risk.
